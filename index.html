Abstract
Financial asset price movement prediction is inherently challenging due to the non-stationary nature of financial markets, where data distributions shift over time. Existing methods often assume that the market is stationary, which limits their applicability.
To address this, we propose the Market-State Jump Diffusion Framework (MSJD), which models non-stationarity through two key components:
â‘  Explicit Market-State Jump Diffusion Process (EMJD) â€” captures dynamics of diffusion, drift, and jump processes governed by latent market states via stochastic differential equations solved by neural networks.
â‘¡ Implicit Market-State Jump Diffusion Process (IMJD) â€” integrates these components into a multi-modal LLM, enabling predictions across varying market conditions through temporal point encoding and jump diffusion embeddings.
â‘¢ General Modality Synthesizer â€” employs a unified adversarial masking strategy to complete missing modalities and fine-tune the prediction model.
Extensive experiments on real-world stock and cryptocurrency datasets demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms existing approaches in predicting price movements.

Method

Market-State Jump Diffusion Framework

MSJD consists of two stages: a General Modality Synthesizer (MSJD-S) that completes missing data modalities, and a General Jump-Diffusion Point Process (MSJD-M) that combines explicit SDE-based modeling with implicit multi-modal LLM reasoning.

Figure 2. Overview of the MSJD Framework. The MSJD-S module first completes any missing data modalities. These complete data are then input into the MSJD-M module: (1) The EMJD utilizes a Neural SDE to compute the drift, diffusion, and jump terms. (2) The IMJD subsequently uses these terms to reprogram a LLM for the final prediction.

Explicit Market-State Jump Diffusion (EMJD)

A Neural SDE explicitly models market state dynamics through drift, diffusion, and jump processes. Monte Carlo simulation provides supervision without circularity, while a Neural ODE captures latent market state evolution.

Implicit Market-State Jump Diffusion (IMJD)

Integrates jump diffusion information into a multi-modal LLM via temporal point position encoding, a 2D router for modality-process selection, and jump diffusion embeddings that decompose into trend, seasonal, and residual components.

General Modality Synthesizer (GMS)

Handles missing modalities through unified adversarial masking post-training. Uses inner/inter-modality mask strategies and adversarial training between GMS (generator) and IMJD (discriminator).

Implicit Market-State Jump Diffusion Process

The IMJD uses a multi-modal LLM to represent the market-state jump diffusion process implicitly. Temporal point position embedding aligns all modalities along the time dimension. A 2D router selects effective modalities and stochastic processes. The mean embedding is decomposed into trend, seasonal, and residual components â€” mapped to drift (Î¼), diffusion (Ïƒ), and jump (J) respectively â€” modulated by explicit Neural SDE outputs.


A Jump-Aware Attention mechanism dynamically adjusts the attention horizon based on market volatility â€” sharpening focus on recent features during high-volatility regimes and attending to longer-term patterns during stable periods.

Case Study

TSLA Oct 2023 Crash â€” A Black Swan Event

We validate MSJD on the Tesla earnings crash of October 2023, a classic jump-diffusion regime shift where market dynamics abruptly transitioned from a diffusion-dominated phase to a jump-dominated phase.

Why MSJD Succeeds Where Others Fail

Traditional momentum models (LSTM, PatchTST) failed catastrophically, continuing to predict upward trends due to historical inertia. FinGPT identified negative sentiment but predicted only a conservative -2.1% decline.


MSJD accurately forecasted the -8.4% crash (close to the actual -9.3%), reducing prediction error by over 70%.


Physics Channel: The EMJD module's jump intensity Î»(t) surged from 0.05 to 0.92, signaling a regime shift and forcing the model to abandon mean-reverting assumptions.


Semantics Channel: The router dynamically shifted attention from Price History to News Signals during the event, intelligently switching focus to the semantic source of the crash.
